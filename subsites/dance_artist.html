dance artist

Anna Nowak danced with the Company Wayne McGregor between 2008-2016. She took part in all of the Company’s creations, participated in numerous films, art installations and a variety of projects, which gave her an unparalleled opportunity for autonomous creativity at the highest level and an invaluable diversity of experience in her professional development. Before joining CWM she danced in the classically oriented Polish National Ballet and the smaller young neoclassical and contemporary Gran Canaria Ballet. After 16 years of professional dancing and touring internationally, she felt ready to move on to exploring different roles in the art of dance.

The mission of working with ones own body and mind with the physical and intellectual challenge that it demands has an incredible power to create a unique kind of fulfillment. The simple act of embodying the movement, being present in ones own body and overcoming the limitations of the mind whilst investing fully in the awareness of the present moment is a truly enriching act.

La fille Mal Garde

Teatr Wielki, 2003

Anna graduated from Parnell Ballet School in Lodz and she received the special award for ‘the Best Graduate’ of Ballet schools in Poland in 2001 as well as a special prize from the Minister of Education for the highest achievements in academics in the same year. Anna did her MA in Art at the Chopin University of Music in Warsaw, specializing in Dance and Dance Teaching.

Anna danced with the Polish National Ballet for six seasons, performing several solo roles in the company repertoire. She danced in all of the classical repertoire, including solo roles in ‘La Bayadere’, ‘Swan Lake’, ‘Sleeping Beauty”, ‘Nutcracker’, ‘La Fille Mal Garde’, ‘Romeo and Juliette’ and others. With the Polish National Ballet she performed works of George Balanchine, Boris Ejfman, Ijri Kylian and other neoclassical and contemporary choreographies.
