dance artist

During her international career Anna Nowak has performed in the most prestigious venues and festivals in the world, including the USA, Canada, Mexico, Australia, Asia and most European countries. Anna danced with the Company Wayne McGregor between 2008-2016 taking part in all of the Company’s creations. She participated in numerous films, art installations and a variety of projects, which gave her an unparalleled opportunity for autonomous creativity at the highest level and an invaluable diversity of experience in her professional development. Before joining CWM Anna danced with the Polish National Ballet dancing several solo roles in the classical repertoire of the company. After 16 years of full-time professional dancing and touring internationally, she felt ready to move on to exploring different roles in the art of dance.

Andrea Mohin / The New York Times

Anna perceives movement as an integral part of her life, which is transcending and informing each and every experience of the ‘dance of life’ like an imprint. Working with ones own body and mind, being constantly exposed to the physical and intellectual challenge that it demands creates a unique kind of fulfillment. The act of embodying the movement, being present in ones own body and overcoming the limitations of the mind whilst investing fully in the awareness of the present moment is a truly enriching act.

Atomos, Wayne McGregor

Ravi Deepres

When Anna experienced the feeling and the process of working on a contemporary choreography for the first time, she never looked back. This was during the creation of “Few Short Sequences’ by Jacek Przybylowicz (Warsaw, 2005). This newly discovered freedom and sense of authenticity has informed her dancing as well as her interests as a Dance Artist. Deepening her resolve to work creatively, be intellectually challenged and perform the kind of work, which allows a sense of exploration and risk she left PNB and started her international career. Initially moving to Spain, she then found her way to London, where she is based since January 2008. Anna is now making her own work and she loves working on the other side of the curtain and watching the piece come together. 

Entity, Wayne McGregor

Yassiek

Anna graduated from Parnell Ballet School in Lodz and she received the special award for the Best Graduate of Ballet schools in Poland in 2001 as well as a special prize from the Minister of Education for her achievements in academics in the same year. Anna did her MA in Art at the Chopin University of Music in Warsaw, specializing in Dance and Dance Teaching. During her six seasons with the Polish National Ballet she danced in a wide range of repertoire, including ‘La Bayadere’, ‘Swan Lake’, ‘Sleeping Beauty”, ‘Nutcracker’, ‘La Fille Mal Garde’, ‘Romeo and Juliette’ and works of George Balanchine, Boris Ejfman, Ijri Kylian, Jacek Przybylowicz and other neoclassical and contemporary choreographies.
